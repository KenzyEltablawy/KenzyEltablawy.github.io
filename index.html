STEM Research Projects
GREENADO — Eco-Friendly Structural Prototype
S1 · 2025/2026
Menofia STEM School · Grade 10 · Group 20106 · Team: Kenzy Raafat, Shahd, Salma, Renad
  • Designed and built a scale prototype house using entirely natural and sustainable materials — reeds (primary structural unit), wool (insulation layer), and palm branches — directly addressing Egypt's Grand Challenge of climate-resilient, affordable housing.
  • Engineering targets: interior temperature maintained below 4 °C above ambient, roof load resistance of 5 kg, wind and rain resilience, fire resistance, and full repairability using locally sourced materials only.
  • Prototype passed thermal management, static load, and lateral load tests across all four structural walls; demonstrated strong thermal stability and structural integrity under controlled test conditions.
  • Validated that eco-friendly, naturally sourced materials can replace conventional building inputs while fully satisfying structural and environmental design specifications.
  • Keywords: Reeds · Wool · Palm Branches · Thermal Insulation · Sustainable Construction · Egypt Grand Challenges
GALVINO — Galvanic Cell Energy Generation
S2 · 2026
Menofia STEM School · Group 20104 · Team: Kenzy Raafat, Shahd, Jana, Radwa
  • Designed and constructed a 12-cell galvanic cell array in series using graphite electrodes sourced from recycled batteries, copper sulfate (CuSO₄) at the cathode, and magnesium sulfate (MgSO₄) at the anode, connected via NaCl salt bridges.
  • Generated over 150 V of electrical output within 2 minutes; ranked among the top teams in the competition phase. Achieved 12.5% energy conversion efficiency — a confirmed 10% improvement over the baseline design.
  • Performed electrochemical analysis: plotted voltage vs. concentration curves (MgSO₄, 20–120 mL), identified optimal electrolyte ratio, and applied Faraday's Law to calculate electron charge transfer.
  • Demonstrated a practical, low-cost electrochemical renewable energy system built entirely from recycled and sustainable materials, with real-world application potential for off-grid energy generation.
  • Keywords: Galvanic Cell · Graphite · Magnesium Sulfate · Redox Reactions · Salt Bridge · Electrochemistry · Recycled Materials
